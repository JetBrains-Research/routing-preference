<BASIS>
Score 5 — Excellent quality
The solution is clean, well-structured, and easy to follow. Naming is clear, logic is organized, complex parts are documented, and the style is consistent.

Score 4 — Good quality, minor issues
The solution is generally well-written but has one or two minor issues, such as an unclear variable name, a missing comment on a non-obvious block, or a small deviation from the codebase's conventions. These do not meaningfully affect readability or maintainability.

Score 3 — Acceptable quality, noticeable issues
The solution is readable but has noticeable gaps across one or more dimensions. For example: logic that requires significant effort to follow, missing documentation on complex sections, or inconsistent naming and formatting throughout.

Score 2 — Poor quality, significant issues
The solution has significant quality problems that make it difficult to read, understand, or maintain.

Score 1 — Unacceptable quality
The solution is largely incomprehensible or unmaintainable. This includes severely inconsistent formatting, complete absence of documentation on complex logic, deeply unclear naming, or structure that obscures rather than communicates intent.
</BASIS>
